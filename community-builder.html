Wellesley Wushu
Community Builder

(Work in Progress)

Background

In the past year of remote practices due to COVID-19, many members had a difficult time persisting with Wellesley Wushu. Experienced members expressed boredom and a lack of motivation due to repetitive practices and newer members described difficulty feeling a sense of belonging. However, members who persisted had a strong sense of community. This project aims to build mental and physical resilience within Wushu by enhancing the community as a whole. Especially now that practices are, in person, it is important for experienced and new members alike to get to know each other. Not only is it more enjoyable to go to practice when you like the people you'll see, but it will help people persist with Wushu.

Initial Designs

Initially, we created two different designs and collected feedback.

The homepage of this design is meant to be inviting, without being overwhelming. It features a personalized greeting, with the user’s name included on the homepage. It offers buttons for the three main features of the app: lunch buddy program, photo scavenger hunt, and collaborative Wushu practice. The design’s photo scavenger hunt page is aimed at allowing the user to quickly view all submitted photos. Similarly, the collaborative Wushu practice pages are designed to provide a simple presentation of the available videos to make it easy to interact with the content. The lunch buddy pages are designed to be a fast and visually straightforward way to sign up, schedule, or view your meeting time.

The homepage of this design is meant to incorporate more interaction with the app to keep the user engaged. It features a welcome message and an invitation to use the “carousel of fun”, where you can use the carousel feature to toggle between each activity in the app: lunch buddy program, photo scavenger hunt, and collaborative Wushu practice. The design’s photo scavenger hunt page is designed to mimic common social picture sharing apps (such as instagram) with a vertical scrolling feature so that the format is familiar to the user. Similarly, the collaborative Wushu practice pages are designed to facilitate more interaction with each individual video, featuring a carousel of the uploaded videos. We added more personalization to the lunch buddy pages for this design by incorporating the lunch buddy’s profile picture. There are also buttons for texting or emailing the user’s lunch buddy so that it immediately opens a text/email draft with their contact information. This is so that our app can minimize the effort it takes to contact and make plans with the lunch buddy.
